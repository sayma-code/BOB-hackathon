Implementation Strategy

Moderate Scenario (Recommended)

Send 3.05 million additional workers abroad over 10 years, increasing remittances by $8.74 billion annually.

Key Components

1. Institutional Framework

Investment: $500M

A. Bureau of Manpower, Employment and Training (BMET)

  • Expand capacity to process 500K workers/year
  • Digitalize all processes
  • Establish overseas offices in 20 countries
  • Create job matching platform

B. Technical Training Centers (100 centers)

  • Construction skills (masonry, plumbing, electrical)
  • Hospitality training (cooking, housekeeping)
  • Healthcare (nursing, caregiving)
  • IT & digital skills
  • Capacity: 300,000 trainees/year

C. Language Training Centers (50 centers)

  • English (for Europe, North America, Australia)
  • Arabic (for Middle East)
  • Japanese & Korean (for East Asia)
  • Capacity: 200,000 trainees/year

2. Training Programs by Destination

Investment: $1.2B

Middle East Programs (200K/year)

  • Construction skills: 80K
  • Hospitality: 60K
  • Domestic work: 40K
  • Driving & logistics: 20K
  • Duration: 3-6 months
  • Cost: $300-500 per person

Europe Programs (150K/year)

  • Nursing & caregiving: 80K
  • IT & digital: 30K
  • Skilled trades: 25K
  • Hospitality: 15K
  • Duration: 6-12 months
  • Cost: $1,000-2,000 per person

North America Programs (100K/year)

  • IT professionals: 50K
  • Healthcare: 30K
  • Engineering: 20K
  • Duration: 12-24 months
  • Cost: $2,000-5,000 per person

3. Financial Support Mechanisms

Fund Size: $1B

A. Migration Loan Program

  • Low-interest loans (5% APR)
  • Cover training, visa, travel costs
  • Loan amount: $500-3,000
  • Repayment: 12-24 months
  • Target: 200,000 loans/year

B. Skill Development Fund

  • Subsidize 50% of training costs
  • Free training for poorest 20%
  • Scholarship for high-potential candidates

C. Pre-departure Support

  • Visa processing assistance
  • Medical checkups
  • Travel arrangements
  • Orientation programs

4. Bilateral Agreements & Partnerships

Target: 30 countries

Priority Countries for G2G Agreements:

  • Middle East: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar (expand quotas)
  • Europe: Germany, UK, Italy (nursing, skilled workers)
  • East Asia: Japan, South Korea (technical intern programs)
  • North America: Canada (skilled migration pathways)
  • Australia: Skilled migration agreements

Benefits of G2G Agreements:

  • Guaranteed job placements
  • Better worker protection
  • Standardized wages
  • Reduced recruitment costs
  • Legal framework for disputes

5. Worker Protection Mechanisms

Investment: $200M

A. Pre-departure Orientation

  • Rights and responsibilities
  • Cultural orientation
  • Contract understanding
  • Emergency contacts

B. Welfare Funds

  • Emergency repatriation fund
  • Legal assistance fund
  • Death/disability insurance

C. Overseas Labor Wings

  • Establish in 20 major destination countries
  • 24/7 helpline
  • Complaint resolution
  • Legal support
