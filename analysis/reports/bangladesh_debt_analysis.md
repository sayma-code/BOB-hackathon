## Executive Summary

Bangladesh's external debt has grown from $15.60 billion in 2000 to $104.49 billion in 2024, 
representing a 569.8% increase over 24 years.

The debt-to-GDP ratio stands at 23.21% in 2024, which is within manageable levels but requires careful monitoring.


## Key Findings

### 1. Debt Growth Trajectory
- External debt increased by 569.8% from 2000 to 2024
- Average annual growth rate: 23.7%
- Accelerated growth observed post-2015 due to major infrastructure projects

### 2. Debt Sustainability
- Current debt service ratio: 18.26% of exports
- Above the sustainable threshold of 15%, indicating pressure on foreign exchange
- Debt servicing costs have increased significantly in recent years

### 3. Economic Context
- Average GDP growth (2000-2024): 5.96%
- Persistent trade deficits throughout the period
- Currency depreciation has increased debt burden in local terms


## Root Causes of Debt Accumulation

### 1. Infrastructure Development
- Large-scale projects: Padma Bridge, Dhaka Metro Rail, power plants
- Necessary for economic development but financed through external borrowing
- Long gestation periods before returns materialize

### 2. Persistent Trade Deficits
- Trade deficits in 25 out of 25 years
- Heavy reliance on imported fuel, machinery, and raw materials
- Export concentration in garment sector (limited diversification)

### 3. Currency Depreciation
- Taka depreciated by 121.7% against USD (2000-2024)
- Increases the local currency cost of servicing USD-denominated debt

### 4. Economic Shocks
- COVID-19 pandemic impact (2020)
- Global commodity price volatility
- Climate-related disasters affecting agriculture and infrastructure

- Improve export competitiveness through infrastructure and skills development
- Negotiate favorable trade agreements with key partners
- Support SMEs in export-oriented sectors

### Priority 2: Import Substitution

- Develop domestic energy sources (solar, wind, hydroelectric)

- Improve agricultural productivity to reduce food imports
- Invest in technology and innovation

### Priority 3: Revenue Enhancement
**Target**: Increase tax-to-GDP ratio by 3 percentage points
- Modernize tax administration with digital systems

- Rationalize tax incentives and exemptions

### Priority 4: Debt Management
- Negotiate debt restructuring with major creditors
- Seek extended repayment periods and lower interest rates
- Maximize concessional financing and grants
- Improve debt management capacity

### Priority 5: Foreign Exchange Management
- Rebuild foreign reserves to comfortable levels (6+ months of imports)

- Facilitate remittance inflows
