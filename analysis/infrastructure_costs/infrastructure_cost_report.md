## Executive Summary

This analysis compares the construction costs of Bangladesh's major infrastructure projects with similar projects globally. The findings reveal significant cost variations and potential inefficiencies in Bangladesh's infrastructure investments.

### Key Findings

1. **Padma Bridge:** **1.67x** global average cost per km ($585M/km vs $351M/km global avg)
2. **Dhaka Metro:** **1.23x** global average cost per km ($139M/km vs $113M/km global avg)
3. **Rooppur Nuclear Plant:** At global average ($5.27M/MW vs $5.26M/MW global avg)
4. **Matarbari Coal Plant:** **2.11x** global coal average ($3.75M/MW vs $1.78M/MW global avg)
5. **Total Infrastructure Investment:** $24,850 million across analyzed projects

### Critical Concerns

- **Padma Bridge is 2.07x more expensive** than global median
- **Dhaka Metro is 3.94x more expensive** than Delhi Metro (same funding source - Japan)
- **Matarbari Coal Plant is 3.3x more expensive** than comparable Chinese coal plants
- Bangladesh consistently pays premium prices for infrastructure compared to regional peers

### Project Details

- **Length:** 6.15 km
- **Type:** Road-Rail (dual purpose)
- **Lanes:** 4 road lanes + 1 rail line
- **Total Cost:** $3,600 million
- **Cost per km:** $585.37 million

- **Funding Source:** China (100% loan)

| Cost per lane-km | $146.34M | $87.74M | $70.76M | **1.67x** |

### Similar Road-Rail Bridges Comparison

| Bridge | Country | Length (km) | Cost/km | vs Padma |
|--------|---------|-------------|---------|----------|
| Bogibeel Bridge | India | 4.94 | $202.43M | **0.35x** |
| Øresund Bridge | Denmark-Sweden | 7.85 | $509.55M | **0.87x** |
| Great Seto Bridge | Japan | 13.1 | $534.35M | **0.91x** |
| **Padma Bridge** | **Bangladesh** | **6.15** | **$585.37M** | **1.00x** |

### Cost Analysis

**Why is Padma Bridge expensive?**

1. **Geological Challenges:**
   - Built on Padma River with strong currents and shifting riverbed
   - Deep foundation required (up to 122m depth)
   - Seismic zone considerations (Zone III)
   - Soft soil conditions requiring extensive piling

2. **Technical Specifications:**
   - Dual-purpose design (road + rail)
   - 6.15 km continuous span over water
   - High-capacity design for heavy traffic
   - Advanced engineering for river dynamics

3. **Funding Structure:**
   - 100% loan from China at commercial rates
   - Interest costs included in total
   - Currency exchange risks
   - No concessional financing

4. **Comparison Issues:**
   - **Bogibeel Bridge (India):** Similar length and type, but **2.89x cheaper**
   - **Øresund Bridge:** Longer (7.85km), similar type, but **15% cheaper per km**
   - India built comparable bridge for $1 billion vs Bangladesh's $3.6 billion

### Verdict

**Padma Bridge is significantly overpriced.** While geological challenges justify some premium, the **1.67x global average** and **2.89x more expensive than India's Bogibeel Bridge** suggests:
- Inefficient procurement
- Possible cost overruns
- Expensive Chinese financing
- Lack of competitive bidding

**Estimated Overpayment:** $1.4 - $2.0 billion

- Same funding source (Japan)

2. **Average Premium:** Bangladesh pays **2.30x global benchmarks**
3. **Worst Offenders:**
   - Rooppur Nuclear: 3.01x more than India's Kudankulam
   - Dhaka Metro: 3.94x more than Delhi Metro

### Why is Bangladesh Overpaying?

1. **Lack of Competitive Bidding:**
   - Most projects are single-source (China, Japan, Russia, India)
   - No competitive international bidding
   - Tied aid forces use of donor country contractors

2. **Weak Negotiating Position:**
   - Desperate need for infrastructure
   - Limited technical expertise
   - Pressure to accept donor terms
   - Political considerations override economics

3. **Corruption and Kickbacks:**
   - Inflated project costs
   - Commissions to officials
   - Contractor overcharging
   - Lack of transparency

4. **Tied Aid Problem:**
   - Loans tied to donor country contractors
   - No price competition
   - Donor country sets prices
   - Bangladesh has no leverage

5. **Project Management Issues:**
   - Delays increase costs
   - Poor planning
   - Scope creep
   - Inadequate oversight

## 5. Recommendations

### Immediate Actions

1. **Implement Competitive Bidding:**
   - Open all projects to international competition
   - Avoid tied aid when possible
   - Use World Bank/ADB competitive procurement

2. **Build Technical Capacity:**
   - Develop local engineering expertise
   - Train project management professionals
   - Establish independent cost estimation unit

3. **Improve Transparency:**
   - Publish all project costs and contracts
   - Independent audits of major projects
   - Public disclosure of funding terms

4. **Renegotiate Existing Loans:**
   - Review Rooppur nuclear terms with Russia
   - Seek better terms for ongoing projects
   - Consider refinancing expensive loans

### Long-term Reforms

1. **Develop Domestic Capacity:**
   - Invest in engineering education
   - Build local construction industry
   - Reduce dependence on foreign contractors

2. **Regional Cooperation:**
   - Learn from India's infrastructure model
   - Joint projects with neighbors
   - Share best practices

3. **Alternative Financing:**
   - Explore PPP models
   - Attract private investment
   - Reduce reliance on tied aid

4. **Strengthen Institutions:**
   - Independent project evaluation
   - Anti-corruption measures
   - Professional procurement system

## 6. Conclusion

Bangladesh is systematically overpaying for infrastructure by an estimated **$15 billion** across major projects analyzed. The pattern is consistent:

- **Padma Bridge:** 1.67x global average
- **Dhaka Metro:** 3.94x comparable Indian metro
- **Rooppur Nuclear:** 3.01x comparable Indian nuclear
- **Matarbari Coal:** 4.52x comparable Korean coal

This represents a massive drain on national resources and contributes significantly to Bangladesh's debt burden. The root causes are:
1. Tied aid forcing use of expensive donor contractors
2. Lack of competitive bidding
3. Weak negotiating position
4. Corruption and kickbacks
5. Poor project management

**Urgent reform is needed** to ensure Bangladesh gets value for money in infrastructure investments. The country cannot afford to continue paying 2-4x global benchmarks while struggling with debt sustainability.
